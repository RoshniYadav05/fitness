~ The treadmill allows a user to walk or run in place on the machine on a belt that moves underneath the feet in a continuous loop.

~ All aspects of the workout can be controlled by the user: speed, incline, warm up period, cool down period, and energy spend.

~ The treadmill has a predictable surface that is much easier to negotiate than sidewalks, curbs or trails and the risk of tripping is reduced.

Hanging leg raise

~ The hanging knee raise is among the best exercises for targeting the lower abs while working out your entire core. This exercise also builds excellent forearm and grip strength.

~ Performing hanging knee raises with proper technique counteracts the tendency of the lower back to arch by strengthening the muscles that oppose this movement.

Dumbbells

Horizontal seated leg press

~ The horizontal leg press is beginner-friendly and simple to operate, making it an excellent choice for individuals new to weightlifting.

~ The reclined position provides more support to the lower back, reducing the risk of strain or injury.

~ The weight is more directly applied to the legs, potentially increasing the effectiveness of the exercise.

~ The 45-degree angle engages more muscles in the lower body, including the glutes and hamstrings.

Lat pulldown

~ The seated cable row is a pulling exercise that works the back muscles. This machine also works the forearm muscles and the upper arm muscles.

~ Lat pull downs are a highly effective exercise for improving upper body strength.

~ They work the muscles of the back, shoulders, arms, and core, and can help to improve posture, stability, and grip strength.
